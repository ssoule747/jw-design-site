The Challenges You Know

01

Four Job Sites. Zero Shared View.

With a 200-room Marriott, the Hotel AVA, a winery, and a mixed-use building running simultaneously, project status lives in different project managers' heads. There's no single dashboard where Derek can see every project's budget health, schedule position, and open issues at a glance.

02

Dozens of Subs. One Big Spreadsheet.

JW coordinates dozens of subcontractors across multiple active sites — electrical, plumbing, HVAC, framing, concrete, finish trades. Insurance expirations, schedule conflicts, and RFI responses are tracked through phone calls, emails, and spreadsheets that no one person can keep current.

03 Core Challenge

Open-Book Budgeting Without the Open Book.

JW promises 'honest open-book budgeting' — it's a core value. But delivering on that promise today means manually exporting spreadsheets into PDF reports and emailing them to clients. By the time the client reads it, the numbers have changed.

04

48 Years of Knowledge Walking Out the Door.

Jerry Williams ran 350+ projects with a system that lived in his experience — which subs to trust, which clients need hand-holding, which budget categories always run over on wineries. As Derek takes the lead, that institutional knowledge needs to live in a platform, not a person.

05

350 Projects. Zero Reviews.

JW has completed over 350 projects with a high repeat client rate — yet they have zero reviews on BuildZoom, zero on Yelp, and no visible Google review profile. The window to capture a testimonial closes within weeks of project completion.

348 projects completed. Repeat clients who've trusted you for 20+ years. A preconstruction process that competitors can't match. The question isn't whether JW Design & Construction delivers — it's whether your systems reflect the standard you've already set.
